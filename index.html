Biological Brain

The original neural network

The human brain — 1.4 kg of tissue — contains more connections than stars in the Milky Way and remains the most complex structure ever studied.

01 / STRUCTURE

Neurons

Specialised excitable cells transmitting electrical and chemical signals. Each has dendrites, a soma, and an axon that fires all-or-nothing action potentials at up to 120 m/s — the fundamental unit of neural computation.

~86 billion in the human brain
02 / CONNECTIVITY

Synapses

Junctions where signals pass via neurotransmitters. Synaptic strength changes through Hebbian plasticity — "neurons that fire together, wire together" — the cellular mechanism behind all learning and long-term memory.

~100 trillion connections
03 / SUPPORT

Glial Cells

Non-neuronal support cells outnumbering neurons 10:1. Astrocytes regulate synaptic chemistry, microglia act as immune sentinels, and oligodendrocytes form the myelin sheath that speeds signal conduction up to 100-fold.

10× more than neurons
04 / ANATOMY

Brain Regions

The cerebrum handles conscious thought across four lobes. The cerebellum coordinates movement. The brainstem controls autonomic functions. The hippocampus and amygdala govern memory and emotion in the limbic system.

4 lobes in the cerebral cortex
05 / CHEMISTRY

Neurotransmitters

Glutamate (excitatory) and GABA (inhibitory) are the workhorses. Dopamine modulates reward, serotonin regulates mood, and acetylcholine drives memory and neuromuscular activation across 100+ known types.

100+ known types
06 / ADAPTATION

Neuroplasticity

The brain's lifelong capacity to reorganise by forming new connections. Long-term potentiation (LTP) strengthens repeatedly activated pathways, underpinning learning, skill acquisition, and recovery from neurological injury.

Continues throughout life

Input

Dendrites

Branch-like extensions receiving incoming signals from other neurons via synapses. The wider the dendritic tree, the more inputs the neuron can integrate simultaneously.

Processing

Cell Body (Soma)

The metabolic hub of the neuron. Integrates all incoming signals — if the combined input exceeds ~−55 mV, an action potential is triggered at the axon hillock.

Transmission

Axon

A long fibre carrying the electrical signal away from the soma. Can extend up to 1 metre in motor neurons. Wrapped in myelin sheath segments separated by Nodes of Ranvier.

Insulation

Myelin Sheath

Fatty insulation formed by oligodendrocytes (CNS). Enables saltatory conduction — signals jump between Nodes of Ranvier, boosting speed up to 100× compared to unmyelinated fibres.

Output

Axon Terminal

When an action potential arrives, vesicles release neurotransmitters into the synaptic cleft, passing the signal to the dendrites of the next neuron and completing the relay.

Layers & Weights

ANNs organise nodes in layers: input, hidden, and output. Each connection carries a learnable weight. A node computes a weighted sum then applies a non-linear activation like ReLU or sigmoid.

Learning

Backpropagation

The network predicts, compares to ground truth via a loss function, then propagates error backward — computing gradients via the chain rule and updating weights via gradient descent. Analogous to synaptic strengthening.

Biology × AI

ML in Bioinformatics

Deep learning predicts 3D protein structures (AlphaFold2), classifies cancer subtypes from genomics data, identifies drug-target interactions, and analyses single-cell RNA sequencing at scale.

Data Mining

Pattern Discovery

Clustering identifies gene expression groups. UMAP and PCA visualise high-dimensional data. Association rule mining finds co-expression networks — extracting biological signal from noise.
